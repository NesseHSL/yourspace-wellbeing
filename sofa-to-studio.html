The Science of Moving Together

Your body is built for community

Before anything else — here's why we built this programme the way we did. Neuroscience tells us something remarkable — when we move in synchrony with other people, something measurably different happens in the brain and body. Mirror neurons fire. Oxytocin is released. Pain tolerance increases. Effort feels easier. Mood lifts higher.

Researchers at Oxford found that people working out in groups had significantly higher pain thresholds than those training alone — not because they were fitter, but because coordinated movement with others triggers the same neurochemical bonding response as physical touch. Your nervous system, quite literally, needs this.

We also know that walking into a studio for the first time can feel genuinely daunting. Not because you don't want to be there. But because standing in a room full of strangers in your leggings, when you're not yet feeling strong or confident in your body, takes a kind of courage that nobody really talks about. Sofa to Studio is designed to build that confidence first. So that by the time you walk through that door, you feel like you belong there.

Sofa to Studio is 30 days. By design. We're not building another app you scroll through forever. We're building a bridge — from your sofa to a studio, a class, a community of real people in a real room. That's where the magic actually happens. This just gets you ready for it.
